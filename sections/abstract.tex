Traffic accidents are one of the main challenges to public safety in Medellín. According to the National Road Safety Observatory (2023), more than 8,200 people died in traffic accidents in Colombia last year, with motorcycles being the most frequently involved vehicle. In Medellín, reports from the Mobility Secretariat indicate that more than 40,000 traffic incidents are recorded annually, including accidents with property damage, injuries, and fatalities. However, this information is often scattered across administrative systems that do not allow for dynamic geospatial analysis or the automated identification of critical areas.

This project proposes the design and development of a digital geospatial analysis platform that allows for the visualization, calculation, and analysis of accident rates using heat maps, interactive graphics, and comparative statistics, in order to support strategic decision-making to mitigate road risk in Medellín. The solution is based on modern software engineering principles, scalable architectures, and urban data analysis.
